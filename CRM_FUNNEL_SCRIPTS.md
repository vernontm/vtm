## The offer ladder (confirmed)

The **click-path tops out at the CRM Lab founding annual ($297).** Above that, Done-For-You is closed by email + a call, not a cold checkout page, because a grand-dollar offer doesn't sell on impulse.

### Click-path (the funnel machine, impulse-sized yeses against a saved card)

| Stage | Page | Offer | Price |
|-------|------|-------|------:|
| 1. Lead magnet | Landing / opt-in | **The Simple CRM Blueprint** — the guide that walks you through building a simple CRM with AI (the stack, the steps, the prompts) | Free |
| 2. Tripwire | Tripwire | **The CRM Build** — the full GitHub repo. Clone the exact CRM I built from scratch | $17 |
| 2b. Order bump | (checkbox on tripwire) | **The Context File** — the file you drop into Claude that knows exactly how the CRM was built; Claude then walks you through every step to the same outcome | +$9 |
| 3. OTO (one-click) | Welcome | **CRM Lab — founding annual.** A full year inside the Lab: I teach you to deploy the build and wire in Gmail, AI, voice calling, and every feature I built into my own CRM, with me live to answer questions. Annual prepaid, one charge, founding rate locked, auto-renews yearly. Includes a capped founding 1:1 onboarding call. | $297/yr |

### Backend (email + call closed, off the click path)

| Stage | Where it sells | Offer | Price |
|-------|------|-------|------:|
| 4. High ticket | "Apply / book a call" card on the welcome page + email sequence + the call. Script 4 is the VSL on that page and the spine of the sales email. | **Done-For-You CRM** — my team builds your custom CRM and hands it over on a call. Includes a full year of CRM Lab. | $997 |
| 5. Continuity (fallback) | Email + a pricing page, for people who didn't take the founding annual at the OTO. | **CRM Lab — monthly.** Same membership, billed monthly. Makes the $297 annual the obvious better deal (roughly 3 months free). | $37/mo |

**CRM Lab, one membership, two ways to buy it.** The OTO pushes the founding annual ($297, one clean charge, one-click friendly). The $37/mo monthly is the fallback sold by email to anyone who passed on the annual. Annual auto-renews at the locked founding rate. *(Note: "Build It With Me" no longer exists as a separate one-time offer. The hands-on teaching is now what's inside the Lab.)*

**Keep "hands-on with me" scalable.** Inside the Lab, access is a group format that scales to any number of members: live office hours, a community space, and a growing library of integration walkthroughs (Gmail, AI, voice calling, each VTM feature). The only 1:1 is a single founding onboarding call, capped to early members, so selling well never becomes a calendar trap.

**Why $997 is off the click path:** $17, +$9, and one-click $297 are reflex purchases against a card already on file. $997 is not a reflex, it needs trust, a conversation, and a beat to think. A surprise grand-dollar checkout right after a $17 buy converts poorly and can sour the experience. So DFY is application + book-a-call, nurtured by email.

**Done-For-You credit rule:** DFY already includes a full year of CRM Lab, so if the contact already bought the **CRM Lab founding annual ($297)**, the Done-For-You offer credits it: **$700** ($997 − $297), with a "your Lab year is already applied" line. Their total across both never exceeds $997. This is a **credit, not a discount** (the Lab they paid for is rolled into DFY), so it doesn't erode the price floor. The funnel tags buyers in Supabase, so the booking page detects the prior purchase and swaps the price + CTA automatically.

**Audience:** blended — (1) solopreneurs and small businesses paying hundreds a month for a CRM they'll never own, and (2) people who always wanted to build their own software with AI but figured they needed to be a "real" developer.

**Proof anchor (the "mom story" equivalent):** Ray built this entire CRM from scratch with AI and filmed every step. The non-technical-person-wins angle: *"I'm not a classically trained developer. I built this whole thing with AI."*

**Voice rules:** no em dashes. Contractions on. Second person. Big-brother energy. Every video closes with some version of *"click the button below... and I'll see you inside."*

## Backend: how the $37/mo monthly and $997 DFY get sold (email + call)

The click-funnel's job ends at the $297 founding-annual one-click. From there, email does the selling: it converts the people who passed on the annual onto the monthly Lab, and it sells DFY. Here are the angles in Ray's voice. These become the email bodies and the booking-page copy. (When we build the funnel, they slot into the `marketing-funnel` skill's email sequence.)

### Sequence A — opt-in / tripwire buyers who did NOT take the founding annual (convert to monthly Lab, then DFY)

1. **Deliver + welcome.** Hand over the blueprint / repo, set the tone, tell them what to do first. No pitch.
2. **The "weekend you'll never get back" email.** The honest cost of wiring it all up alone (Gmail, AI, voice calling). Frame: the Lab is where I show you exactly how. Soft CTA to the monthly Lab.
3. **Proof email.** "I built this whole thing from scratch with AI and filmed every step." Link the build series. Removes the "I could never" objection.
4. **Lab monthly close.** Concrete: here's what's inside, here's a walkthrough I just added. CTA: join the Lab monthly ($37/mo). Remind them the founding annual is the cheaper way if they're in for the year.
5. **The math email (DFY pitch).** The $100/mo = $1,200/yr vs $997-once-and-you-own-it reframe. CTA: book a call. This is script 4 compressed to email length.
6. **Scarcity / close.** "We only take a limited number of builds a month." Last call to book before the slots reset.

### Sequence B — CRM Lab members (annual or monthly): retention + DFY upsell

1. **Onboarding.** Book the founding 1:1 onboarding call (annual members), point them at the first walkthroughs, set the expectation that new modules drop regularly.
2. **Module drop.** Show a real new module/integration just added to the Lab. "This is what being inside looks like." Keeps retention high and renewals sticky.
3. **DFY side-door.** For members who are clearly busy or stuck: "Want me to just build it for you instead? Your Lab year credits toward it." → the $700 credited DFY path.

**Mutual exclusion:** anyone who books DFY gets pulled out of the DFY sales sequence and into DFY client onboarding. DFY already includes a full year of CRM Lab, so DFY clients land in Sequence B (retention), not the Lab sales emails.

### CRM Lab — what's actually inside (so the offer is concrete, not vague)

- Step-by-step teaching to deploy the build and get it live
- Integration walkthroughs: Gmail, AI, voice calling, and every other feature built into the VTM CRM, shown for your own custom build
- New modules and features added regularly
- Live group office hours + community so you're never stuck at midnight alone
- A founding 1:1 onboarding call (capped to early members)
- Founding rate locked for life

## Notes for production

- **Lengths** match the originals (roughly 300 to 450 words each, about 90 seconds to 2 minutes spoken).
- **The reframe metaphors** are the spine of each script, same as the originals: storefront becomes *recipe vs finished meal* (tripwire), and *engine vs knowing how to wire it up* (welcome/OTO).
- **Swap-in points** if you want to localize the proof: anywhere I say "I built this whole thing with AI," you can name-drop the YouTube series ("I filmed the whole build on YouTube") for extra credibility.
- **Pricing is adjustable** without touching the structure. Only the spoken numbers in scripts 3 and 4 change.
- **The GoHighLevel anchor is the headline price comparison** and it's reusable everywhere: GHL runs $99 to $497 a month, forever, and you own nothing, versus $297 once to learn to build and own your own. It lives in the OTO script (3), and it should also headline the landing page, the Lab pricing page, and the Lab monthly-close email (Sequence A4). Same move works against HubSpot and Salesforce if you want variety.
- **The $297 OTO is annual prepaid** (one charge, auto-renews yearly at the locked founding rate). That single-charge model is what keeps it one-click friendly. The $37/mo monthly is a separate Stripe price used only on the email/pricing-page path.
- **The $700 credited DFY path** (script 4 alternate close) needs the booking page to check the contact's buyer tags in Supabase and swap the price + close. On the payment side that's one Stripe price of $700 for tagged Lab-annual buyers vs the standard $997 for everyone else. No manual proration math at checkout.
- When you're ready to build the actual pages, the `marketing-funnel` skill has parameterized opt-in / tripwire / OTO / welcome / high-ticket HTML plus the Stripe + email wiring that this exact ladder maps onto.
