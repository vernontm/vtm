WHAT IS GITHUB? • PLAIN ENGLISH

Think Google Drive... for your app.

GitHub is where your project lives online. Every file, every change, every version, saved forever. If your laptop died right now, your project would still be safe. Want to undo a change from three weeks ago? One click.

It's also the middleman. Vercel watches GitHub. When you save a change, Vercel auto-updates the live site. You don't think about it. It just works.

TODAY'S MISSION › 3 SMALL THINGS

Here's what Step 2 covers in the doc:

1Create your free GitHub account. Same email you used for Supabase and Vercel. About 3 minutes.
2Turn on two-factor auth. 60 seconds. Protects your account forever. Use an authenticator app.
3Create your first repository. Call it my-crm. Make it private. Add a README. Done.

THE WORDS YOU'LL HEAR

Term Plain English
Repository A folder for your project. Each app gets one.
Commit A labeled save point of your work.
Push Uploading your saved work to GitHub.
Branch A safe copy to experiment on.

You don't need to memorize this. AI handles it. This is just so the words stop feeling foreign when you see them.
